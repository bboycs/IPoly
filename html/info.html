5 Most Endangered Coral Reefs in the World

1. The Great Barrier Reef
It is a marine park. Located in Australia. It is the largest reef in the world. The risk of dying by 95% in the year 2050.

2. Southeast Asian Coral Reefs
An oversight that has put the region’s nearly 100,000 square kilometers of coral reefs, almost 34 percent of the world total, in grave danger.

3. Caribbean Coral Reefs
Nearly two-thirds of coral reefs in the Caribbean are threatened by human activities, such as coastal development, sediment and pollution, marine based threats and overfishing.

4. Kingman Reef
Located about 1,000 miles southwest of Honolulu, Kingman Reef is an uninhabited, barren, coral atoll with a deep lagoon. “It became a U.S. naval reservation in 1934.

5. Florida’s Coral Reefs
“Monitoring data from 105 stations in the Florida Keys has revealed a 44 percent decline in coral cover from 1996-2005,” reports the Florida Department of Environmental Protection.
